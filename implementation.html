INTERSTATE V2 — DASHBOARD CARD SPECIFICATION
Developer Reference
Purpose: This tab provides a live reference for Interstate V2 development. Each card below is a working example of the exact KPI card format needed. Every card displays: the current period value, % change vs prior month with directional arrow, gallon/dollar difference vs prior month, % change vs prior year with directional arrow, gallon/dollar difference vs prior year, and YTD cumulative total. Cards glow green when trending up, red when down, and yellow when flat. These cards pull from the same data layer as all other dashboard tabs — when data is imported, these cards update in real-time.
FUEL SUMMARY — AT A GLANCE
These four cards provide the highest-level view of fuel performance across the entire Roady's network. A leadership user should be able to open the dashboard and immediately see total network volume, how aggregators and fleets are performing, and the average output per stop — all with month-over-month and year-over-year context.
AGGREGATORS — AT A GLANCE
Aggregator gallons represent the total fuel volume flowing through third-party aggregator discount programs (QPN, TCS/Apex, Edge, etc.) across all truck stops in the network. This is a critical revenue lever — aggregator volume drives discount revenue and network stickiness.
FLEETS — AT A GLANCE
Fleet gallons represent direct fleet discount volume — trucking companies and carriers with negotiated discount programs at network stops. Fleet relationships are typically higher-volume, higher-loyalty accounts.
FUELMAN — AT A GLANCE
Fuelman is a fleet card payment program. Revenue is generated per-gallon from enrolled truck stops. Key metrics are total gallons processed, number of active enrolled locations, and estimated revenue at the per-gallon rate.
R-CHECK — AT A GLANCE
R-Check is a check cashing / verification service for truck drivers. Revenue is per-transaction from enrolled stops. Metrics mirror Fuelman: total gallons/transactions processed, active locations, and estimated revenue.
REWARDS — AT A GLANCE
The Rewards program generates revenue through three streams: Rewards Fees (charged to locations), Expired Points Revenue (unredeemed loyalty points that expire), and the overall Rewards Fee Revenue. These three cards should appear at the top of any rewards view to give immediate revenue visibility.
VENDOR PROGRAMS — AT A GLANCE
Vendor Programs revenue comes from vendor enrollment fees and program participation across the truck stop network. This card shows total monthly vendor program revenue with full comparison context.
GROWTH STRATEGIST — BY TERRITORY
Each Growth Strategist manages a territory of truck stops across assigned states. These cards break down key revenue and volume metrics by territory, giving leadership immediate visibility into which regions are performing, growing, or need attention. Every GS territory card shows the same variance pattern: vs prior month, vs prior year, and YTD — enabling direct comparison across territories.
